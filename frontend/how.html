how does it work?

why would banks pay me?

Banks are hoping you’ll stick around and use their services. By offering a cash bonus, they’re betting that, over time, they’ll earn more from things like fees, interest, or other products you might use. Think of it like a “welcome mat”: they’re putting out the cash to get you in the door and keep you coming back.

is it really that simple?

Mostly, yes. Switching your account is handled by CASS, a system that makes the process safe and standardised. There are usually a few hoops - like setting up direct debits or leaving your account open for a few months - but they’re not tricky if you can stay organised.

how much money can I make?

You won't become rich! Bonuses usually fall between £100–£200. Still, that’s essentially free cash for following a few simple steps. Once you’ve claimed a bonus, the same bank probably won’t give you another for a few years, so treat it as a one-off win rather than a steady income.
